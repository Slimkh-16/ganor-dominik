The Story

Take One

Once upon a time
a Princess lived a lie,
She wore the flowers in her hair,
With sadness and despair.
The crystal shoe was not her fit
The Damsel dreamed of freedom,
As luck would have it
She got deserted Kingdom.
She danced and laughed
And kicked the earth...
But wait... That doesn't sound right,
There was no Princess
But a charming Knight.

Take Two

Once upon a time
a Knight daydreamed of life,
Where his sublime was not denied
Existence so divine...
That all the Gods would sigh.
With the quote on his foot
He would rescue the weak,
With the face on his boot
The freedom he'd seek.

But wait...
The shoe, the quote, its all a blur...
There was no charming Knight,
A fair child and no Sir,
That sounds almost right.

Take Three

There once lived a child,
So pure and dear,
An angel wild,
With no touch of evil.
The child had a dream:
With each day a new beginning
With the mind so bright A fairytale with winning.
The child refused to stop,
Until the shoes were ruined
For a dream one must fight
Take a deep breath and hold tight.
This tale is madness
Yet, one must agree,
There is not but a clue...
Could this child be you?

by Masha Mitrofanova
